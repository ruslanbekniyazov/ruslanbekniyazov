Microsoft Explore

If I was to rank experiences that contributed the most significant to my learning, my first internship at Microsoft woudl place very high. It was my first exposure to the industry, and I am glad to say that it has been very smooth, thanks to the great culture and thanks to the team that I was a part of. Looking back, it surprises me how much I was able to learn and accomplish in 12 weeks.

Our team of 3 was tasked with developing a gamified full-stack training solution aimed to test the security knowledge of Microsoft employees. We were able to accomplish that using ASP.NET (utilizing MVC design pattern), Unity Engine, WebGL, Azure. We implemented the training solution with a theme and design that we stuck to, added a storyline to make the game more interesting, and implemented leaderboards to track employee progress and incentivize learning.

During the internship, I experienced both the Product Management and Software Engineering disciplines, gaining the skills that I used and will definitely continue using as I move forward.

StartupUW

Seattle is a hub to a huge amount of talent, and I believe that University of Washington should become a center of innovation and startups in the U.S. That is why I joined StartupUW, the biggest entrepreneurship club at UW. Together, starting with a small team of about 8 people, we were able to reorganize the club and split it into intiatives, and me, I was in charge of Startup-a-thon.

Startup-a-thon was an early-stage startup competion, and with hours of work being put into development, planning, and marketing, we were able to host a grandiose event with over 100 participants. From renowned speakers to useful startup workshops, this event was a success.

Becoming more and more indulged in the operations of the club, I became the Executive VP of the club. With the success of Startup-a-thon, we were able to expand the team to almost double the size, after which I progressed to the position of a Co-President. With dedicaiton, we are striving towards making StartupUW the central node for student startups that start at UW Seattle. Click here to check out the club, and the website we built.
