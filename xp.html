Microsoft Explore

If I was to rank experiences that contributed the most significant to my learning, my first internship at Microsoft is amongst the very top. It was my first exposure to the industry, and a very smooth at that, thanks to the great culture to the team that I was a part of. Looking back, it surprises me how much I was able to learn and accomplish in 12 weeks.

Our team of 3 was tasked with developing a gamified full-stack training solution that tests the security knowledge of Microsoft employees. We were able to accomplish that utilizing tools like ASP.NET (MVC), Unity Engine, WebGL, and Azure. We implemented the training solution with a theme and design that we stuck to, added a storyline to make the game more interesting, and implemented leaderboards to track employee progress and incentivize learning.

During the internship, I experienced both the Product Management and Software Engineering disciplines, gaining the skills that I will continue using going forward.

Microsoft SWE Internship

After my Explore internship, I came back to Microsoft as a SWE intern on Yammer's Compliance and Search team. And this time, interning on campus allowed me to meet & become friends with so many new people!

I was able to truly get hands-on with so many principles used at the forefront of tech. Utilizing test-driven approach and comprehensive ways of testing has been core to anything I write. Operating and really understanding Microservice architecture at its core, utilizing event-driven programming, archiving, streaming, Docker and Kubernetes are just some of the skills that I gained that helped me become a better engineer and problem-solver. And overall, it was a truly fun experience.

Amazon SDE Internship

At Amazon, I was on the Alexa AI team working on Natural Language Understanding (NLU). I was able to work on a variety of projects: regression testing, cache optimization, and data handling. I was able to learn a lot about the Alexa ecosystem, and I was able to get hands on experience with AI and Machine Learning. I gained the valuable experience of working with cloud services, and I was able to learn a lot about AWS.

My main project, revolving around improving regression testing, optimized the workflow of data scientist working with Alexa AI from around a day down to mere minutes. My secondary project, revolving around cache optimization, in full implementation was projected to double the efficiency.

iD Tech

Having taught myself a lot of the skills I currently possess, I wanted to support the young Computer Scientists by guiding and teaching them the skills that have been so invaluable to my software and game development processes. I was able to do that through iD Tech, a company that provides online lessons for kids and teens, where they learn about the latest tech and develop their skills in a fun and engaging way. I taught game development using Unity Engine, and due to my success as game development instructor, I was amongst chosen to be among the first instructors at the company to teach Virtual Reality Development.

At iD Tech, what was very important to me is that I was able to learn a ton. Not only by improving my presentation and communication skills, but also by brushing up and learning new things about the concepts that I was teaching, thanks to the amounts of planning and research that went into each lesson.

StartupUW

Seattle is a hub to a huge amount of talent, and I believe that University of Washington should become a center of innovation and startups in the U.S. That is why I joined StartupUW, the biggest entrepreneurship club at UW. Together, starting with a small team of about 8 people we were able to reorganize the club and split it into intiatives. And in the beginning, I was in charge of Startup-a-thon.

Startup-a-thon was an early-stage startup competion, and with hours of work being put into development, planning, and marketing, we were able to host a grandiose event with over 100 participants. From renowned speakers to useful startup workshops, this event was a success.

Becoming more and more indulged in the operations of the club, I became the Executive VP of the club. With the success of Startup-a-thon, we were able to expand the team to almost double the size, after which I progressed to the position of a Co-President. With dedicaiton, we are striving towards making StartupUW the central node for student startups that start at UW Seattle. Click here to check out the club, and the website we built.
